15 Aug 2022 8 Min read

Machine learning is a lot like it sounds: the idea that various forms of technology, including tablets and computers, can learn something based on programming and other data. It looks like a futuristic concept, but this level of technology is used by most people every day. Speech recognition is an excellent example of this. Virtual assistants like Siri and Alexa use the technology to recite reminders, answer questions, and follow commands. As machine learning proliferates, more professionals are pursuing careers as machine learning engineers.

On the other hand, Analytics is more about predicting the potential of future events using Data and Analysis information both. But for the sake of generalizing everything and ease of using terms, let’s consider analysis are also a specialized branch of analytics. Most of the companies would consume only 2 aspects of Data Analytics.

  1. Descriptive Analytics – What Happened
  2. Diagnostic Analytics – Why did it Happen
But Data Science is a field which evolved far away from these aspects. After exploring above 2 stages you can move to the next levels of analytics.

  • Predictive Analytics – What will happen?
    At this stage we would model the possibilities of events that would occur in future. For an example by looking at the number of Covid-19 patience in past few months, we could try to build a model to predicts the future patient numbers in a specific timeline.
  • Prescriptive Analytics – How can we make/not make it happen?
    This about an intelligent model of taking its own decisions and adjusting its parameters accordingly. For an example we can think of a ship line which is modeled to control by an intelligent system which all the ships would stay connected to each other by tracking each other’s movement and the system itself would take the decision of assigning relevant ships to travel different regions by considering different parameters in the most efficient manner. And here we come the second level of clashing the norms in the industry. The company HR manager expects the Data Scientist to perform as the Data Scientist, Data Analyst, Data Engineer, Data Architect and even as the Business Analyst. Even though these fields show highly interconnected, technical wise there are specific disciplines for each of these roles.

Data Analyst – Data Analyst would be the person who would plan all the means and methods to gather the relevant information and Data that is needed to make data driven predictions. ·
Data Scientist – The key role of Data Scientist would be cross functioning with IT teams, building up statistical and mathematical models which would predict the future business action plans. A Data Scientist would need to have an understanding on Statistics, Mathematics, Computer fundamentals, and Machine Learning.

Data Engineer – Data Engineers would carry on the work on the foundation laid by Data Scientists. They would convert the algorithms to prototype models, ease the Data Accessibility and create the Dashboards, reports and tools for users.

Data Architect – Data Architect would be responsible for maintaining the relational Databases in the company and visualizing Data for end-users in the company.
